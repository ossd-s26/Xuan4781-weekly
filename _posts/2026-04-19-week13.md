### Open Source Isn't Just for Software

When one thinks of open source this far in, we usually think of software. It's hard to not think of software but in fact there are a lot of open source projects that aren't software based. The one I chose to look into was **Hugging Face**, which is basically a hub for open source machine learning models and datasets. What stood out to me is that it's not just one company's thing, thousands of researchers and developers are uploading, sharing, and improving models together. It's crazy that what started out as an ad on social media turned out to be something I'd use if I want to build a cool website. Anyone can grab a model, fine-tune it, and push it back. Funny enough, this is software, so we're not going to talk about it today.

### Mozilla Common Voice

The true non-software project that genuinely surprised me was **Mozilla Common Voice**. The idea is simple but powerful, it's where people donate their voice clips so that speech recognition technology can be trained on real, diverse voices. It's completely open and anyone can contribute. I didn't realize how much of a problem it actually is that most voice datasets are proprietary and dominated by certain accents or demographics. So if you ever wondered about the accents the voices like Siri or Alexa. These are just based on private data owned by big companies. 

So for this open source project, you're literally lending your voice to a dataset that developers around the world will use. That's a very different kind of open source contribution compared to submitting a PR.
